Del Carmen vs. General Luna: Choosing Your Siargao Base

Two destinations in Siargao, Philippines, two completely different experiences. Find your perfect match.

Published: January 1, 2025 • Updated: April 26, 2026
See Mariden Resort rooms →

Disclosure: This guide is published by Mariden Resort. While we've aimed for accuracy, we encourage you to research both areas for your trip.

Siargao Island in the Philippines has two distinct personalities: the energetic, surf-centric vibe of General Luna (GL) and the laid-back, nature-focused charm of Del Carmen . Choosing where to stay depends entirely on your travel style, budget, and timeline. This honest 2026 comparison will help you make the right decision, or better yet, convince you to experience both. Quick Answer: Where Should You Stay in Siargao?

  • Best Siargao area for first-time international visitors: Del Carmen, closest to Sayak Airport, quieter, and cheaper.
  • Best area for surfers: General Luna, with walking distance to Cloud 9 and other surf breaks.
  • Best area for families with kids in Siargao: Del Carmen, with calmer roads, kid-friendly Sugba Lagoon and Mangrove Boardwalk.
  • Cheapest place to stay in Siargao: Del Carmen, 20-40% cheaper than General Luna for mid-range rooms.
  • Best area for digital nomads in the Philippines: Del Carmen with Starlink, or General Luna for coworking and café scene.
  • Best for nightlife in Siargao: General Luna, with bars, live music, late-night food.
  • Best Siargao base near Sugba Lagoon: Del Carmen, same municipality as the departure port.

✅ Pros of General Luna

  • World-famous Cloud 9 surf break right on your doorstep
  • Endless restaurants, cafés, and international cuisine options
  • Vibrant nightlife with bars and live music
  • Easy to meet other travelers and backpackers
  • Lots of surf schools and board rentals
  • The popular Tri-Island Hopping Tour (Naked Island, Daku Island, Guyam Island) departs from General Luna (from Del Carmen, add 30-45 minutes travel time to reach the departure point)

❌ Cons of General Luna

  • Crowded: Especially during peak season and weekends
  • Expensive: Tourist markup on everything from food to accommodation
  • Noisy: Late-night parties, motorbikes, construction
  • Traffic: The main road gets congested, especially during sunset
  • Distance from airport: ~45 minutes from the airport (~25 km by shared van)

✅ Pros of Del Carmen

  • Gateway to turquoise lagoon in Siargao Be first on the water every morning
  • ~5 minutes from airport to Mariden Resort (~3 km) Zero travel stress on arrival
  • Peaceful and quiet: Perfect for relaxation and sleep
  • More affordable: Local prices, not tourist markup

  • Access to unique attractions: Sugba Lagoon rates and timing , free mangrove forest walk , Alegria Beach family-safe swimming , Taktak Falls waterfall guide , Magpupungko low-tide rock pools , Pacifico Beach surf guide , Sohoton Cove tour guide , palm-fringed lagoon in Malinao , and Maasin River kayak tour
  • Better for families with children

❌ Cons of Del Carmen

  • Limited nightlife (restaurants close earlier)
  • Del Carmen has a small but growing food scene; expect local carinderias and a handful of restaurants rather than the dozens of international options in GL. For variety, plan occasional dinner trips to GL (30-45 min ride).
  • 30-45 minutes (~30 km) from Cloud 9 surfing
  • Quieter social scene (great or bad depending on you!)
  • ATM Availability: Del Carmen has one ATM (Cantilan Bank). General Luna has BDO and other ATMs, though all Siargao ATMs can run out of cash during peak season. Bring sufficient cash from the mainland.

Frequently Asked Questions

It depends on your travel style. Del Carmen is ideal for nature lovers, families, couples, and digital nomads seeking peace and proximity to Sugba Lagoon. General Luna is better for surfers, party-goers, and solo travelers looking for nightlife and social scenes. Many visitors choose to split their stay between both.

Accommodation and dining in Del Carmen are generally 20-40% cheaper than equivalent options in General Luna, especially for mid-range stays. Budget options in both areas can be comparable. See our Siargao daily cost breakdown for exact figures.

Approximately 30-45 minutes (~30 km) by motorbike or van (see Siargao transport rates ). The road is scenic and well-maintained.

Del Carmen is significantly better for families. It is quieter, more affordable, has less traffic, and offers kid-friendly attractions like the sunset mangrove boat tour and the Sugba Lagoon boat tour . General Luna's party atmosphere and congested roads are less suitable for young children.

Del Carmen doesn't have famous surf breaks like Cloud 9, but you can easily rent a motorbike and ride to General Luna's surf spots when you want to catch waves. Many guests use Del Carmen as a quiet base and make day trips for surfing.

Bring sufficient Philippine Pesos (PHP) from Cebu, Manila, or your home country. Siargao has limited ATMs (one in Del Carmen, a few in General Luna) and they frequently run out of cash during peak season. Most tours, carinderias, and tricycles are cash-only. International cards work at a few GL restaurants and resorts, but don't rely on them.

Globe and Smart both cover Siargao but signal strength varies by barangay. For reliable work-grade internet, stay somewhere with Starlink ; coverage on local mobile towers can drop during storms and peak tourist hours. Mariden Resort in Del Carmen runs Starlink with solar backup so you stay online through outages.
