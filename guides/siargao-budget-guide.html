How Much Does a Siargao Trip Cost? Budget Breakdown (2026)

Daily costs in PHP & USD for budget, mid-range, and comfortable travel, plus how Del Carmen saves you 30–40%

Published: January 23, 2026 • Updated: April 26, 2026

Disclosure: This guide is published by Mariden Resort. While we've aimed for accuracy, we encourage you to research both areas for your trip.

"How much does a Siargao trip cost?" is the most common question we get from guests planning a holiday on Siargao Island, Philippines, whether they're flying in from Manila, Sydney, London, or New York. The honest answer: it depends on your travel style. Backpackers and long-stay digital nomads can get by on ₱700/day ($12 USD) ; couples on a surfing trip or island-hopping holiday usually sit at ₱2,000–3,500/day ($33–58 USD) ; families wanting comfort spend ₱4,000–6,000/day ($67–100 USD) . This guide breaks down every expense category in both PHP and USD so you can plan accurately. Pro tip: Staying in Del Carmen instead of General Luna can save you 30–40% on your entire trip.

USD estimates in this guide use ~₱56–₱58 = $1 USD (typical 2026 range). Check live rates before you travel.

11 Money-Saving Tips

Accommodation

  1. Stay in Del Carmen (30-40% cheaper than GL) - Same attractions, less tourist markup. Note: This guide is written from the perspective of staying in Del Carmen (where Mariden Resort is located), so it naturally highlights the advantages of that part of the island. Most of the island’s restaurants, nightlife, and surf schools are in General Luna, which is about 30 km (roughly 45–60 minutes by motorbike) from Del Carmen.
  2. Book direct with resort - Avoid OTA fees, often includes perks like airport pickup
  3. Travel off-peak (June-November) - Many people consider June–November to be the off-peak or rainy season in Siargao, with fewer tourists and lower prices. However, Siargao’s weather is quite unpredictable, and heavy rain and storms are more likely from October onwards. Siargao doesn’t have a true dry season, and you can expect some rain year-round.
  4. Stay longer for weekly discounts - Most resorts offer 10-20% off for 7+ nights

Food

  1. Eat at carinderias - In more local carinderias, expect to spend around ₱80–150 (~$1.36-$2.54 USD) per meal in 2025–2026 vs ₱200-300 (~$3-$5 USD) at tourist spots
  2. Buy food supplies in Dapa (major savings!) - Dapa has the cheapest prices on the island. Stock up on water, snacks, and meals for your day trips. Bring containers to pack food - saves 30-50% compared to buying at tourist spots
  3. Buy from local markets - Fresh produce, fish at local prices
  4. Share meals - Portions are often big enough for two
  5. Drink local beer, not imported - San Miguel vs imported saves ₱50+ (~$0.85+ USD)/bottle

Activities & Transport

  1. Do free activities - free mangrove forest walk , beach hopping, sunset watching
  2. Share tour costs - Find other travelers to split boat tours like Sugba Lagoon from Del Carmen (₱1,600-2,150 (~$27-$36 USD) boat divided by 6 people = ₱265-360 (~$4-$6 USD) each) or caves and lagoons tour (Sohoton)

The Biggest Savings: Staying in Del Carmen instead of General Luna can save you 30-40% on accommodation, food, and transport - without sacrificing any Siargao experiences!

Payment, ATMs & Money Tips

Cash is King

  • Most places are cash-only
  • ATMs available in GL (limited in Del Carmen)
  • Bring enough cash for 3-4 days minimum
  • International card ATM fees are roughly ₱250 (~$4 USD) per withdrawal.

Where to Withdraw

  • BDO - General Luna (branch/ATMs on or near Tourism Road)
  • Cantilan Bank - General Luna, Del Carmen, and Dapa
  • PNB (Philippine National Bank) - Dapa
  • Euronet ATMs - multiple in General Luna. Note: Euronet ATMs are easy to find but usually charge higher fees than local bank ATMs, and travelers have reported occasional issues, so use them mainly as a backup.

💡 Tip: Withdraw larger amounts (₱10,000-20,000, ~$169-$339 USD) to minimize the ₱250 (~$4 USD) ATM fee. Many places are still cash-only, and ATMs sometimes run out of money on weekends. Bring enough cash for several days, and consider setting up GCash or Maya for QR payments at cafes and shops that support them. See our getting around Siargao guide for detailed fare information.

Frequently Asked Questions

A week in Siargao costs ₱12,000-18,000 ($200-300 USD) for budget travelers, ₱22,000-32,000 ($367-533 USD) for mid-range, and ₱35,000-50,000 ($583-833 USD) for comfortable travel. This includes accommodation, food, activities, and transport.

Siargao is moderately priced compared to other Philippine destinations. General Luna (the tourist hub) has higher prices, but Del Carmen offers 30-40% savings on accommodation and food. Budget travelers can spend as little as ₱700/day ($12 USD).

Stay in Del Carmen instead of General Luna (30-40% cheaper), eat at local carinderias (₱80-150/meal, ~$1.36-$2.54 USD), do free activities like the Del Carmen mangrove boardwalk and beaches, rent a motorbike to share costs, and visit during off-peak season (June-November) for accommodation discounts.

Del Carmen is significantly cheaper. Mid-range rooms cost ₱1,500-2,000/night (~$25-$34 USD) vs ₱2,500-4,000 (~$42-$68 USD) in GL. Local meals are ₱80-150 (~$1.36-$2.54 USD) vs ₱150-250 (~$2.54-$4.24 USD) in GL. Overall, expect 30-40% savings by staying in Del Carmen. See our comparison guide for details.

Sugba Lagoon boat tour joiner tours typically cost ₱1,500-1,900/person (~$25-$32 USD). Naked, Daku, Guyam island hopping joiner tours start around ₱1,500/person (~$25 USD). Sohoton Cove tour guide tours run roughly ₱2,500-3,100/person (~$42-$53 USD). Surf lessons cost ₱500-800/hour (~$8-$14 USD). Many free or low-cost attractions are available like Magpupungko low-tide rock pools (₱60-100, ~$1-$2 USD, entrance) and easy waterfall day trip from Del Carmen (Taktak Falls, around ₱30, ~$0.51 USD).

Cash is king in Siargao. Most local restaurants, shops, and tour operators only accept cash. ATMs are available in General Luna and Dapa, but have ₱250 (~$4 USD) fees per withdrawal for international cards. Bring enough cash for 3-4 days minimum and withdraw larger amounts to minimize fees.

Bring at least ₱10,000–20,000 ($180–360 USD) in cash for your first few days. ATMs in General Luna and Dapa charge ₱250 (~$4 USD) per withdrawal for international cards and sometimes run out of cash on weekends. GCash and Maya QR payments are accepted at some cafes and shops but not everywhere.

Siargao is generally cheaper than Bali's tourist areas (Seminyak, Ubud) and comparable to Thailand's islands. Budget travelers can spend ₱700–1,200/day ($12–20 USD) in Siargao. The biggest savings come from staying in Del Carmen instead of the tourist hub of General Luna.

Tipping is not expected in Siargao but is appreciated. For tour guides and boat operators, ₱50–100 ($1–2 USD) per person is a kind gesture. Restaurants do not typically add a service charge, and rounding up the bill is common. There is no obligation to tip tricycle or habal-habal drivers.
