Magpupungko Rock Pools is one of the most photographed natural wonders in the Philippines, a stunning formation of tidal rock pools that reveal themselves during low tide along the rugged east coast of Pilar, on Siargao Island. Whether you're flying in from Manila, Cebu, or directly from the US, UK, Australia, or Canada, Magpupungko is one of the must-visit stops on any Siargao itinerary . This guide covers tide schedules, entrance fees (₱50-100 / ~$1-2 USD), what to bring, visa and currency basics for international visitors, and how to experience this bucket-list attraction from your Del Carmen base camp at Mariden Resort — 30 minutes away by motorbike.

Unlike the increasingly crowded surf towns of General Luna, Magpupungko sits on Siargao's quieter eastern coastline, where natural beauty takes precedence over rapid development, and the slow-paced island vibe still thrives. For Filipino travelers it's a classic East Coast day trip; for international visitors it's the Philippines at its most undeveloped.

What Are the Magpupungko Rock Pools?

Magpupungko (pronounced "mag-poo-POONG-ko") is a natural formation of limestone and volcanic rock pools carved by centuries of waves and tides along Pilar's eastern coastline. During low tide , the ocean recedes to reveal crystal-clear pools of varying depths - from ankle-deep wading pools perfect for kids to deeper basins (3-4.5 meters) ideal for swimming and cliff jumping.

The name "Magpupungko" comes from the Visayan word for "sitting" or "crouching," referring to the rock formations that appear to crouch along the shore.

Why Magpupungko Stands Out

  • Tidal Pools as Natural Infinity Pools: The pools blend seamlessly with the ocean horizon, creating stunning photography opportunities
  • Cliff Jumping Platforms: Multiple jumping heights (3-5 meters) for thrill-seekers
  • Untouched East Coast Setting: Part of the quieter, less developed eastern region - no mega-resorts or party crowds
  • Natural Wonder with Easy Access: Paved pathway to the beach area, though reaching the actual pools requires wading through water over sharp rocks

When to Visit: Understanding the Tides

CRITICAL: Magpupungko Rock Pools are only accessible during low tide . The tide schedule changes daily, so timing is everything.

Tide Timing Essentials

  • Low Tide Window: Pools are visible and swimmable approximately 2 hours before and after the lowest tide
  • High Tide: Pools are completely submerged by ocean waves - not safe or accessible
  • Check Daily Schedules: Ask your resort, local guides, or use tide prediction apps (search "Siargao tide schedule")
    Tide Tools: Siargao Tide Forecast | Tide Charts Siargao App
  • Best Months: Dry season (March-October) offers calmer seas and better weather

Recommended Visiting Times

Morning Low Tide (6AM-9AM):

  • ✅ Fewer crowds, better light for photography
  • ✅ Cooler temperatures
  • ❌ May require very early departure from Del Carmen

Midday Low Tide (10AM-1PM):

  • ✅ Convenient timing, easier logistics
  • ✅ Warmer water
  • ❌ More crowded (especially weekends/holidays)
  • ❌ Harsh overhead sun for photos

Afternoon Low Tide (2PM-5PM):

  • ✅ Golden hour photography potential
  • ✅ Moderate crowds
  • ❌ May rush to return before dark

💡 Pro Tip: Aim for weekday mornings during the dry season (March-October) for the most peaceful experience. Avoid November-January when the east coast experiences heavy rainfall and strong waves that can make pools inaccessible or dangerous.

How to Get There from Del Carmen

Magpupungko is located in Pilar municipality on the east coast of Siargao - perfectly accessible from Del Carmen without the long drive from General Luna.

Motorcycle Route (Recommended)

From Mariden Resort, Del Carmen:

  1. Head north on the main coastal road (Siargao Circumferential Road)
  2. Pass through Pilar town proper (20 minutes)
  3. Continue to Barangay Consolacion (10-15 minutes more)
  4. Follow signage to "Magpupungko Rock Pools" - well-marked
  5. Park at the designated area (₱20-30, ~$0.34-$0.51 USD, parking fee)
  6. Walk 5-10 minutes down a paved path to the beach/cottage area
  7. Wade through water and walk over sharp rocks to reach the actual pools (wear water shoes)

Total Travel Time: 30-35 minutes
Road Condition: Mostly paved, some rough patches - standard motorcycle suitable. For rental rates and tips, see our tricycle and scooter rental rates .

Tricycle/Van Option

  • Tricycle from Del Carmen: ₱600-800 (~$11-14 USD) roundtrip with waiting time
  • Private van / habal-habal: Negotiate; typically ₱1,200-1,500 (~$21-27 USD) for a group

What to Bring

Essentials

  • ☑️ Swimwear - quick-dry preferred
  • ☑️ Water shoes or reef sandals - rocky surfaces
  • ☑️ Reef-safe sunscreen - protect the marine ecosystem
  • ☑️ Towel - for drying off after swimming
  • ☑️ Waterproof phone case or dry bag - protect electronics from splashes
  • ☑️ Cash - for entrance fee, parking, cottage rental, snacks (no ATMs nearby)
  • ☑️ Water bottle - stay hydrated (limited options on-site)

Optional But Recommended

  • 📷 Action camera/GoPro - capture underwater and pool shots
  • 🥽 Snorkel gear - spot fish and coral in deeper pools
  • 🧢 Hat/cap - sun protection during waiting periods
  • 🍫 Snacks - on-site food limited and pricier
  • 💊 First aid kit - bandages for minor scrapes on rocks

Tips for the Best Experience

🌊 Timing is Everything

Check the tide schedule the night before and plan your day around low tide. Apps like "Tide Times Siargao" or ask locals at your resort for accurate schedules.

📸 Photography Pro Tips

  • Best Angle: Stand on the outer rocks during calm low tide for the "infinity pool" shot
  • Golden Hour: If low tide coincides with sunrise (6-7AM) or sunset (5-6PM), you'll get magical lighting
  • Avoid Crowds: Weekday mornings = fewer people in your shots
  • Use Portrait Mode: Capture the depth and layers of pools, rocks, and ocean

🏝️ Combine with Other North Attractions

Make it a full east coast adventure. Since all three beaches are connected by the scenic coastal road, you can create the perfect day trip:

  1. Morning: Start south at hidden Secret Beach (Guiwan) for calm swimming (20 min south of Pacifico)
  2. Midday: surf at Pacifico Beach for surfing or lunch with ocean views
  3. Afternoon: Magpupungko Rock Pools (10 min north of Pacifico, time it with low tide)
  4. Sunset: Stay at Magpupungko for golden hour photography over the pools
  5. Optional: Stop at Alegria Beach (Del Carmen) on the way back

🗺️ East Coast Route: This is Siargao's most scenic coastal drive. The route Secret Beach Malinao → Pacifico surf beach → Magpupungko showcases three completely different beach experiences in one unforgettable day.

Visiting Magpupungko from the US, UK, Australia & Canada

Magpupungko Rock Pools rank among the Philippines' most photographed natural attractions on TikTok and Instagram, drawing travellers from the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and Canada year-round. If you're flying in from New York, London, Sydney, or Toronto, here's what you need to know before you go:

  • Getting to Siargao: Fly into Sayak (IAO) Airport from Manila (MNL) or Cebu (CEB), 2 to 3 hours total from most international arrivals. See our Sayak Airport arrival guide .
  • Visa: US, UK, AU, and CA passport holders enter the Philippines visa-free for up to 30 days. Passport must be valid for 6+ months.
  • Language: English is widely spoken in Siargao tourism areas; signage, menus, and tours are available in English.
  • Currency: Philippine Peso (₱ / PHP). Bring cash for Magpupungko; no card payments on-site. ATMs available in Del Carmen and General Luna, not at the pools.
  • Connectivity: Grab a local SIM (Globe or Smart) at the airport for mobile data Essential for tide apps and maps.
  • Safety: Siargao is considered one of the safer islands in the Philippines for tourists. Standard travel precautions apply.
  • Best months for international visitors: March-October (dry season) aligns with US/UK/AU/CA summer and shoulder travel windows.

East Coast Siargao: Quieter and More Authentic

Magpupungko represents what makes Siargao's east coast special - a region that has resisted the rapid development sweeping through General Luna and Cloud 9. Here's why the eastern side of the island remains Siargao's most authentic:

Preserving Island Culture

  • Local-First Tourism: Entrance fees support Barangay Consolacion, not foreign investors
  • Small-Scale Development: No mega-resorts or commercialized infrastructure
  • Community-Managed: Pools maintained by local families who've lived here for generations
  • Authentic Interactions: Vendors and guides are neighbors, not corporate employees

Why Del Carmen is Your Strategic Base

Staying in Del Carmen positions you perfectly to explore the east coast without sacrificing comfort:

  • 30 minutes to Magpupungko vs. 45-60 minutes from GL
  • Access to both coasts: East (Magpupungko, Pacifico) and west (Sugba Lagoon, Sohoton)
  • Lower costs: Del Carmen resorts are 30-50% cheaper than GL equivalents
  • Authentic experience: Live among locals, not tourist crowds
