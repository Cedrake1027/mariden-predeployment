Siargao Itinerary, Philippines: Sample Trip Plans for Every Traveler

Day-by-day plans for 3, 5, and 7-day Siargao trips in the Philippines, from weekend escapes to full-week adventures

Planning a trip to Siargao Island, Philippines , but unsure how to structure your days? We've built four sample itineraries (a 3-day quick escape, a balanced 5–7 day stay, a family-friendly plan, and an adventure-seeker route) to help first-time international visitors and returning Filipino travellers get the most out of the island. Each plan is designed around Del Carmen as a convenient base: 5–10 minutes from Siargao Airport (IAO), close to Sugba Lagoon, and well-placed for day trips to Magpupungko, Pacifico, Taktak Falls, and General Luna. 3-Day Siargao Itinerary: Quick Weekend Escape

Best For: Weekend travelers, short layovers, first-timers with limited time

Siargao Airport (IAO) is a domestic airport located in Del Carmen; most travelers fly in via Manila or Cebu, and routes can change seasonally.

Day 1: Arrival & Settle In

  • Morning/Afternoon: Arrive at Siargao Airport (IAO) — arrivals & transfer guide
  • Transfer: Mariden Resort is about 4 km from the airport, which is roughly a 5–10 minute ride by tricycle or car, depending on traffic and road conditions. Typical tricycle fares are around ₱200 (~$3.60 USD), but prices can vary by driver and season. (Airport pickup ₱200/transfer).
  • Check in and freshen up

Late Afternoon Options:

  • Option A: Relax by the pool and recover from travel
  • Option B: Quick visit to Mangrove Boardwalk near Del Carmen (about 10 minutes away by tricycle; expect a small environmental or entrance fee)

Evening: Dinner at a local carinderia near the resort (₱100–150 / ~$2–3 USD)

Day 1 Estimated Cost: ₱300–500 / ~$5–9 USD (excluding accommodation)

Day 2: Sugba Lagoon Adventure

  • 7:00 AM: Light breakfast at resort
  • 8:00 AM: Depart for Sugba Lagoon — Del Carmen's signature boat tour . From Del Carmen port, the boat ride to Sugba Lagoon typically takes around 30–45 minutes, depending on boat speed and weather conditions. In high season, consider booking popular tours like Sugba Lagoon at least a day in advance.
  • 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM: Swimming, diving platform, paddleboard/kayak. Most destinations such as Sugba Lagoon charge small environmental fees collected on-site, usually not included in the tour price.
  • 12:00 PM: Lunch at the lagoon (included in some tours, or around ₱200–300 / ~$4–6 USD)
  • 3:00 PM: Return to Del Carmen
  • 4:00 PM: Rest at resort, pool time
  • 6:00 PM: Sunset drinks
  • 7:00 PM: Dinner at resort or nearby restaurant

Day 2 Estimated Cost: ₱1,800–2,500 / ~$33–45 USD (As of 2026, typical join-in Sugba Lagoon tours are around ₱1,500–2,000 / ~$27–36 USD per person, depending on season and inclusions. Always confirm current rates when booking.)

Day 3: North Exploration & Departure

Option A (Morning Flight):

  • 6:00 AM - Breakfast
  • 7:00 AM - Airport transfer (roughly 5–10 minutes)
  • Depart Siargao

Option B (Afternoon/Evening Flight):

  • 7:00 AM - Early breakfast
  • 8:00 AM - Maasin River & Coconut Viewdeck (about 30 min away) OR Magpupungko Rock Pools (if low tide timing works)
  • 12:00 PM - Return, lunch, pack up
  • 2:00 PM - Airport transfer

Day 3 Estimated Cost: ₱500–1,000 / ~$9–18 USD (depending on activities)

5 to 7-Day Siargao Itinerary: The Full Island Experience

Best For: Typical tourists wanting the "full Siargao experience"

Day 1: Arrival & Del Carmen Base

  • Arrive at Siargao Airport, transfer to Mariden Resort (roughly a 5–10 minute ride) - see our Sayak Airport arrival guide for arrival tips
  • Afternoon: Pool & rest, recover from travel
  • Evening: Explore Del Carmen town, local dinner

Day 2: Sugba Lagoon Day Trip

  • Full day Sugba Lagoon rates and timing tour
  • Swimming, kayaking, cliff jumping from the diving platform
  • Return late afternoon

Day 3: East Coast Adventure

  • Morning: Magpupungko Rock Pools on Siargao's east coast (check tide schedule!)
  • Lunch: Pilar town
  • Afternoon: Big Wish surf spot (surf or swim)
  • Return to Del Carmen

Day 4: General Luna Day Trip

  • Rent motorbike (₱400–500 / ~$7–9 USD per day) or hire habal-habal (see our Siargao transport costs guide for rental tips)
  • Morning: Explore GL, breakfast at tourist cafe
  • Midday: Cloud 9 boardwalk, watch surfers
  • Afternoon: Surf lesson (optional, ₱500–800 / ~$9–15 USD per hour)
  • Evening: GL nightlife OR return to Del Carmen

Day 5: Island Hopping (Tri-Island Tour)

  • Book through GL tour operators
  • Visit Naked Island, Daku Island, and Guyam Island (Tri-Island Tour)
  • Full day tour. Tri-Island join-in tours usually cost around ₱1,500–2,000 / ~$27–36 USD per person, but prices vary with season, group size, and what's included. Expect small environmental fees on some islands.
  • Return to Del Carmen evening

Day 6: Nature & Relaxation

  • Morning: Siargao jungle waterfall (Taktak Falls) hike
  • Afternoon: coconut palm road in Maasin (photos!)
  • Evening: Pool, pack, final dinner

Day 7: Departure

  • Morning activity if time permits (e.g., sunset mangrove boat tour )
  • Airport transfer (roughly 5–10 minutes from Del Carmen)

Family-Friendly Siargao Itinerary (Any Duration)

Best For: Families with children, cautious travelers. (Tip: See our best month to visit Siargao guide for weather planning).

Family-Friendly Principles

  • ☑️ Calm, shallow water activities
  • ☑️ Short travel distances
  • ☑️ Flexible timing (nap time!)
  • ☑️ Kid-friendly food options
  • ☑️ Avoid rough surf/deep water

Recommended Activities for Families

Best for Families:

  • ✅ Mariden Resort Pool - Safe, supervised, no currents
  • ✅ free mangrove forest walk - Easy walk, educational, shaded
  • ✅ Alegria Beach family-safe swimming - Calm, shallow, local families swim here
  • ✅ kayak and SUP in Maasin - Calm water, rope swing, shallow areas
  • ✅ book a Sugba Lagoon boat - Calm lagoon, life jackets provided. Sugba Lagoon is generally considered family-friendly, but children and less-confident swimmers should always be supervised, and parents should follow the instructions of local guides.

With Caution:

  • ⚠️ Magpupungko entrance fee and times - Only during very low tide, watch kids closely
  • ⚠️ Taktak Falls waterfall guide - Water levels and conditions at Taktak Falls can change with the season and recent rainfall. Paths may be slippery, so wear proper footwear and supervise children closely.

Avoid with Young Kids:

  • ❌ Cloud 9/Surfing - Strong currents. For young children and less-confident swimmers, it's best to enjoy surf spots like Cloud 9 and Pacifico from the shore or through supervised lessons, as waves and currents can be strong.
  • ❌ tri-island tour from General Luna - Long boat ride, sun exposure
  • ❌ palm-fringed lagoon in Malinao - Rocky access

Sample 5-Day Family Itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrival + Pool Day - Settle in, let kids adjust, pool time, early dinner
  • Day 2: Mangrove & Beach - Morning at the Philippines' largest mangrove forest (1-2 hours), afternoon at quiet Siargao beach for families (Alegria, calm and shallow)
  • Day 3: Sugba Lagoon - Family-friendly tour, bring snacks and sun protection. Kids love the diving platform!
  • Day 4: Flex Day - Pool morning, Maasin coconut plantation guide afternoon (rope swing!)
  • Day 5: Departure - Easy morning, breakfast, airport (roughly a 5–10 minute transfer)

Family Budget Considerations

  • Mariden Family Room: ₱3,500–4,500 / ~$64–82 USD per night (fits 4)
  • Kid-friendly food widely available
  • Some attractions and tour operators offer reduced rates for children, but this isn't universal, so always check when booking.
  • Bring: Reef shoes, rash guards, snacks, sunscreen

Frequently Asked Questions

We recommend 5-7 days for the full Siargao experience. This allows time for Sugba Lagoon, island hopping, Magpupungko Rock Pools, surfing, and relaxation. If you're short on time, 3 days is enough for highlights like Sugba Lagoon and one major activity.

Start with 2-3 days in Del Carmen (near the airport) to visit Sugba Lagoon, Mangrove Boardwalk, and Magpupungko. Then spend 2-3 days in General Luna for surfing, island hopping, and nightlife. Return to Del Carmen for easy airport access on departure day.

Yes! Del Carmen is particularly family-friendly with calm attractions like Sugba Lagoon, Mangrove Boardwalk, Alegria Beach, and Maasin River. Avoid Cloud 9 surfing (strong currents) and rocky beaches. Stay at resorts with pools for safe swimming.

As rough estimates for 2026, very budget-conscious travelers who stay in simple guesthouses or dorms, eat mostly at local eateries, and limit paid tours might spend around ₱1,000–1,500 / ~$18–27 USD per day. Many mid-range visitors, who prefer private rooms and join-in tours, spend about ₱2,000–3,500 / ~$36–64 USD per day excluding flights. Actual costs vary with season, room sharing, and how many tours you do. See our Siargao daily trip cost guide for detailed breakdowns.

Del Carmen is ideal for families, nature lovers, and those wanting peace and is often cheaper than General Luna, particularly for accommodation. General Luna suits surfers and party-goers. Many visitors split their stay between both areas to experience everything Siargao offers. Read our Del Carmen vs GL comparison for a detailed breakdown.

There are no direct flights to Siargao from the US, UK, Australia, or Canada. Most international travellers fly into Manila (MNL) or Cebu (CEB) first, then take a 1–2 hour domestic flight to Siargao Airport (IAO) via airlines such as Cebu Pacific or Philippine Airlines. Plan at least 3–4 hours between your international arrival and domestic departure, especially in Manila where terminal transfers are required.

Siargao uses the Philippine peso (PHP). Most tours, tricycles, and small restaurants are cash-only. ATMs exist in General Luna and Del Carmen but can run out of cash during peak season — it's wise to withdraw in Manila or Cebu before flying in. USD is not widely accepted for day-to-day purchases; exchange to PHP on arrival or at major airports.
